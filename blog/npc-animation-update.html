Pixel Art & NPC Animation Milestone

June 16, 2026 Development Update

This week was a major art and systems push. I've replaced the tilesets with a completely updated set, expanded my art concepts for multiple environments, and made significant progress on the Stealth Camping system that sits at the heart of Park & Hide.

New Tilesets

The original tileset was functional but didn't quite capture the cozy, lived-in feeling I wanted. The new set brings cohesion across all environments: pine forests, grasslands, rocky outcrops, and water features all feel like they belong to the same world.

I've added more variation in grass, trees, and ground details so no two campsites feel identical. The tiling is tighter, the palette is more refined, and the overall vibe is much closer to what I imagined.

Environment Concepts

Beyond the tilesets, I've been exploring different biome concepts to keep the gameplay fresh:

  • Pine forests with dense tree coverage (your stealth advantage)
  • Open meadows (high visibility, higher risk)
  • Rocky terrain with natural cover spots
  • Lakeside campsites with unique resource gathering
  • Seasonal variants that affect gameplay and NPC behavior

Each environment tells a story and presents different tactical challenges for the stealth camping loop.

Stealth Camping System Deep Dive

The core gameplay loop is coming together. Site selection now factors in visibility, proximity to water and resources, and NPC patrol patterns. Once you set up camp, you need to manage your stealth — noise discipline, fire management, and timing your activities around patrol schedules.

The system rewards patience and observation. Rushing means getting caught. Waiting means finding the perfect moment to gather resources, cook meals, or interact with NPCs without drawing attention.

Animated Tilesets in Godot

Getting animated tilesets working in Godot was a real struggle at first — between atlas setup, animation frames, and how Godot's TileSet editor expects everything to be wired together, it took a while to wrap my head around it. A few YouTube tutorials later, it finally clicked, and I got my first animated beaches running.

What's Next

I'm integrating the new tilesets fully and building out a few more environment types. The NPC roster is growing, and I'm refining how their daily schedules interact with your camping patterns. The goal is a living, breathing world that reacts to your choices.

Thanks for following the journey. More updates soon.
